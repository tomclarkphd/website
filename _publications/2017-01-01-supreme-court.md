This book examines how the broader political climate frames legal issues and shapes the coalitions that emerge among the justices. It builds on a series of articles Clark published with Benjamin Lauderdale that proposed models for measuring ideology from voting behavior. Published in the Cambridge Series in the Political Economy of Institutions and Decisions.
